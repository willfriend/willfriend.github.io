Traktable

Putting Science to Work for Peace

Learn more

Cutting edge social science research is changing how we understand and resolve conflicts.

For the past 30 years, research from fields as various as Political Economy to Cognitive Neuroscience has yielded profound insights for understanding and resolving conflict across contexts - from business disputes to civil wars. Despite this progress, most of these insights have remained hidden from the public knowledge, tucked away in labs and the literature.

At Traktable, we put research insights into practice to resolve conflict across contexts

Corporate Negotiation and Dispute Resolution

Using research in Cognitive Science and Negotiation Practice

Political Conflict and Deadlock

Using research in Political Psychology and Economic Game Theory

Global Intractable Conflict

Using research in Socio-Psychological Dynamics and Behavioral Neuroscience

Peace Treaties and Post Conflict Systems

Using research in Political Science and Dynamical Systems Theory

At Traktable, our thesis is simple: Conflict Resolution is about value.

Destructive conflict destroys value while the process of succesful resolution creates value. As a for-profit in a sea of NGO’s, we focus on delivering this value to our consumers rather than courting donors.

At the societal level, peace generates massive economic and social value for citizenry, businesses and governments alike. We believe there is a latent demand for efficacy in peacebuilding and conflict resolution endeavors for which all three of these actors are willing to pay.

In the private sector, mishandled conflict can poison corporate culture, derail negotiations and incur regulatory punishment. In all three of these areas – intra-business, inter-business and in the regulatory environment – businesses have a huge incentive to be at the cutting edge of conflict knowledge.

At Traktable, we aim to meet both of these demands. We believe that for-profit status allows us to be more effective than traditional NGO’s and nonprofits. However, experience also shows that problems can arise between competing profit and social welfare motives. We aim to counter this through incentive design and radical transparency of our financial information and scientific data. We will try our best, but it would be dishonest to say that profit and social good motives will never come into competition. If we do stray from our mission, our open data should allow our customers and the public to see and fight back. We want to actually make the world a more peaceful place and actually make money, not either or. If this all sounds unconventionally honest for a company intro, then all the better.

We are not a conventional company. We do not intend to become one.

Radical Transparency

All financial information will be publically available and easily accesible in the languages of each country we work in. All of the data and rationales we use to make our decisions will be publically released. We will gather data on our performance to see what works and what doesn't and improve accordingly.

Data + HUMINT

In the development sector, there is a split between those who see data and those who see stakeholder participation as respective panaceas. Realizing the promises and pitfalls of both, we temper empirically driven methods with constant communication with people in and effected by conflict to ensure that we are achieving efficacy on the ground.

Local Integration

We reject the aloofness of the Davos class. We want to physically work in the areas which we do business. Beyond hiring local, this means investing in the economic infrastructure and human capital of where we work to create real long term relationships and win community buy-in. We embrace unorthodox partners and unorthodox benefits to achieve results.

Individual Fulfillment

We want the best work culture on earth. Period. This means offering more oppurtunities for adventure and meaningful work than any other employer. It also means hiring those who - beyond possesing exceptional talent and singular experiences - are also one-in-a-million joys to work with.

Our People

Will Friend

Founder

Will Friend is a student at Brown University ('20) studying an independent concentration in Strategy, Economics and Conflict (SEC). His interest in conflict first piqued by a youthful roadtrip through the Northern and Southern United States, he has gone on to work with UNESCO and in the University of Pennsylvania's Peace and Conflict Neuroscience Lab (PCNL). He is passionate about combining different disciplinary modes - foremost neurocognitive, socio-psychological and political methods - in order to improve conflict resolution processes across a variety of contexts.
